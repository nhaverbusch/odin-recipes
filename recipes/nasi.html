Nasi Goreng with a fried egg!

Description

Making your own nasi is not difficult at all and you certainly don't need any packets or bags of nasi spices. It does require quite a list of ingredients, but don't let that put you off.. I make my nasi goreng according to this recipe over and over again and I continue to enjoy it!

This dish is for 4 persons!

Ingredients:

  • 300 grams of brown rice
  • 2 onions
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 leek
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 cm fresh ginger (djahé)
  • 1 teaspoon trassi (shrimp paste)
  • 1/2 red pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sambal (you can have more, I love it!)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon laos (galanga root)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ketoembar (dried coriander)
  • 1/2 stock cube
  • oil/butter for frying
  • salad onion
  • fried onions

Steps:

  1. Cook the rice according to the instructions on the package until tender in a pan of water with a stock cube. Preferably do this well in advance so that the rice can dry well.
  2. Peel and chop the onion. Cut the leek into thin rings and wash them in a colander. Scrape the carrot and cut it into thin strips. Finely chop the red pepper.
  3. Clean the garlic and peel the ginger.
  4. Heat some oil in the frying pan and sauté the onion. Add the red pepper and crush the garlic and ginger. Fry until the onion looks glassy and then add the carrot and leek. Fry for about 5 minutes.
  5. Then add the trassi, sambal, cumin, turmeric, laos and ketoembar. Stir in all the flavors well.
  6. Add the rice for the nasi goreng to the pan with vegetables, stir well and turn up the heat. Add the soy sauce, soy sauce and oyster sauce. Fry the rice for about 5 minutes until well heated. Add some extra sambal, soy sauce or oyster sauce to taste.
  7. Serve the nasi goreng with salad onion, fried onions and of course a fried egg!

Bon appetit!
